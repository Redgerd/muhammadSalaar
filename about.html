About Me

Thanks for dropping by! My name’s Salaar—and yes, that’s spelled with two a's

Speaking of my names, if mine hasn’t already betrayed me the word Salaar means "leader" so no pressure, right?

Long story short, I'm a tech enthusiast with a knack for building data-driven solutions and robust backends. I enjoy turning complex datasets into something meaningful and love optimizing the flow behind the scenes.

Education

I'm currently diving into my Bachelor's in Software Engineering at the Military College of Signals, NUST, which I've been pursuing since 2022

I thrive in collaborative environments that encourage growth, offer varied learning opportunities, and cultivate my problem-solving skills and creative strengths.

These days, I’m mostly focused on web development with React, JavaScript, and CSS, but I also have a solid foundation in general-purpose programming languages like C++, Python, and Java. Some of my interests in web development include typography, performance, and accessibility.

As a self-proclaimed data nerd, I thrive on all things data and strategy!There’s just something cool about building data pipelines and wrangling huge datasets to uncover insights. Whether I’m figuring out how to get data from one place to another or optimizing how it’s stored, I love the challenge of making everything run smoothly.

I won’t bore you too much with my work history, though; that’s what my LinkedIn is for.

Hobbies and Interests

Reading

Every now and then, I’ll dust off a book and do some light reading. I love finding that rare book that captivates me so much that time slips away, where closing it almost feels like waking from a dream. A few of my favorites include:

  • Collected Stories by Gabriel García Márquez
  • The Picture of Dorian Gray by Oscar Wilde
  • Notes from Underground by Fyodor Dostoevsky
  • No Longer Human by Osamu Dazai
  • Letters from a Stoic by Seneca

I don’t have a reading list per se, although there are some books I’d like to one day read… Okay, I guess that’s technically a reading list, but hopefully you get my point. I don’t like to treat reading as something to check off a list because then it feels like work.
